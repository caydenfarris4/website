# The Light Was Already There

*The Work · Vol. 5*

I was standing in a room full of coaches, speakers, and leaders from every corner of the Maxwell world. The band started playing. Hands went up across the room.

I closed my eyes.

And I felt it. That specific warmth in the chest. The one I had felt a thousand times in sacrament meeting, in the temple, on my knees in a tiny apartment in Honduras when a woman I had just taught about Jesus Christ had tears streaming down her face.

The Spirit.

I opened my eyes and looked at the room. Baptists. Methodists. Catholics. Pentecostals. A few Latter-day Saints sprinkled through. All of them singing to the same Savior I worship every Sunday. And the Spirit was there.

Now, I am a member of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. I know where the priesthood keys are. I know what I covenanted in the temple. I know the ordinances that must be performed for a person to return to the presence of God, and I know where those ordinances are administered on the earth today. None of that was shaken in that moment. All of that was confirmed.

But something else was confirmed too.

The light of Christ does not require a tag.

I want you to sit with that for a second. Because I think a lot of us grew up assuming the Spirit only shows up where the ordinances are. That the Spirit is the building's furniture. That outside our walls there is a kind of spiritual dryness, and inside our walls there is water.

It is not true.

Moroni 7 is not subtle about this. *"Every thing which inviteth to do good, and to persuade to believe in Christ, is sent forth by the power and gift of Christ."* Every thing. Not some things. Not the things that happen in our chapels. Every thing.

John Maxwell spent that morning pointing a room of leaders toward Jesus Christ. He did it in his voice, in his tradition, with his language. And the Savior was pleased. Because the Savior does not measure ministry by denomination. He measures it by whether souls are being drawn toward Him.

So what does that mean for me?

It means the question is not whether the Spirit is at work in the world. The Spirit is at work everywhere there is a heart turned toward Christ. The question is what I am doing with the specific light I have been given.

Because here is the other half of this.

I have more than the light of Christ. I have the gift of the Holy Ghost. I have priesthood authority through the restoration. I have covenants I renewed last Sunday and the Sunday before that. I have access to temple ordinances that bind generations. That is not a brag. That is a responsibility.

And if the Spirit can reach into a hotel ballroom through a Baptist worship team, then the Spirit can reach through me in a meeting at Caselle. Through a line in my book. Through a text message to my brother. Through the way I treat a barista who got my order wrong.

Every word I write is a testament that there is a loving Savior.

Every email I send.

Every blog post I publish.

Every sentence of my book.

Every conversation in every hallway.

I do not have to put on a tag and fly back to Honduras to be a missionary. I already served that mission. I am serving a different one now, and it runs through every syllable that leaves my mouth and every keystroke that leaves my hands.

Paul said it this way. *"As much as in me is, I am ready to preach the gospel."* The part of the gospel that is in him. Not the part that is in Peter. Not the part that is in James. The part that is in him.

The part that is in me is specific. It is built from a swimming pool where I earned an alternate slot at state. It is built from a mission in Honduras where I learned a language and how to kneel. It is built from late nights writing a book at twenty-two and early mornings studying scripture and the quiet hours when I sit at my desk and ask the Lord what He wants said next.

That is the instrument He built. That is the voice He gave me.

If I compromise that voice, I fail the people He sent me to reach.

Now, I want you to think about your voice. Not the voice you think you should have. Not the voice of your favorite pastor or your favorite author or your favorite leader. Your voice. The one built from your specific life. The one the Lord shaped through every win and every loss and every quiet moment on your knees.

What if that voice is the instrument He is waiting for you to use?

What if the people He wants to reach through you will never listen to a general sermon, but they will listen to you?

What if every word you spoke, starting tomorrow morning, was a testament that there is a loving Savior, and you wanted to be like Him?

I am not there yet. I miss it often. I say things I wish I could take back. I write sentences that serve me more than they serve the reader. I am still building. Still learning. Still becoming.

But I know what I am aiming at.

I left that worship session more convinced than ever that the Savior is at work in every corner of this world. That the light of Christ is in every person who is trying. That my job is not to compete with that light.

My job is to carry the specific flame He handed me. And to make sure every word I speak adds to the fire already burning.

The light was already there.

My job is to make sure my voice carries it too.
